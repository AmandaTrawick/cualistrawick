Heuristic review

Usabilty Heuristics Obvservation
Visibility of system status There is a loading circle towards the page title.
Match between system and the real world I think the menu labels are easy for the user to understand.
User control and freedom There are several ways to go back to the home page (clicking on the logo or where it says “home” under the menu bar). There isn’t anything on the website that easily lets you go back to the previous page.
Consistency and standards The menu labels are in the same spot on each page. The search bar moves a little from the home page to the other pages, which makes it harder to find on some pages. It is also located in multiple locations. For the most part all the articles are in boxes with an image, title, date, and a few lines of the article. When you click on one of the menu labels, there may be one image at the top of the page followed by a list of titles with some information from the article.
Error prevention It tells you want the error is and has a suggestions. Both can be vauge.
Recognition rather than recall The search bar remembers previous searches if you are using the same device.
Flexibility and efficiency of use The archives on the side provide quick access to articles from a certain month and year. The menu labels at the top of the page provide quick access to articles for that topic.
Aesthetic and minimalist design The general colors of the design are not overpowering. Having a lot of boxes of various sizes, can be a little overwhelming. The archives box is a lot longer than the rest of the information on the page. This makes you scroll for a long time to get to the bottom of the page.
Help users recognize, diagnose, and recover from errors When something is misspelled in the search or there are no search results for the search, it tells you that there are no results and to try again with different terms. It doesn’t offer specific suggestions.
Help and documentation I haven’t seen a FAQ page or anything to help explain how to navigate the page.

Strengths

  • There are pictures for almost every article.
  • The layout of the system is consistent which makes it easy for users to navigate.

Weaknesses

  • It’s not always clear why something is pulled up with a search. Unless it’s in the title or the three lines of the article displayed in the box, it’s not easy to see what makes the article relevant to the search. You have to go into the full article to see if it’s something that you’re looking for.
  • The archives box is long and requires you to scroll for a long time to get to the bottom of the page, where the archives is listed again. Anything listed under the page menu will rarely be looked at, so there is a lot of wasted space.

Part 1 and Part 2 Comparision

There were similar frustrations between parts 1 and 2. When we searched something that was spelled wrong, we were told there were no results and to try again with different terms. It didn’t offer suggestions on what to search instead. Also, it looked for every search term individually. This caused it to pull a lot of articles that were not relevant to the search. We both found it to be well organized and easy to use. There were a few differences. The main difference is the approach we took to reviewing the site. The heuristic analysis is more defined in what I was looking for. The mini-usability test didn’t have as many set points for the person to look at. We looked at different things and focused on different parts of the website. I think improving the search options will help improve the website. Offering suggestions when a search term doesn’t have any results will help when there are misspelled words and help point people in a direction that will show results. I also think the archives box can show the years and expand to show months so the page isn’t so long.
