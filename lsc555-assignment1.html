Information System: The National Archives Acess to Archival Databases (ADD)

System Introduction

Context:

The National Archives’ purpose is to keep the nation’s records. While they only keep about 2-5% of federal records created every year, they permanently keep and preserve what they have. Researchers are able to use the ADD to help further their research.

Users:

The main users of the ADD are researchers.

Content

The National Archives has:

  • 1.5 billion pieces of paper
  • 375,220,691 pages of digitized records
  • 41 million photographs
  • More than 33 billion electronic records (1 petabyte in total)
  • More than 725,000 artifacts
  • 40 million aerial images
  • More than 450 million feet of film
  • 10 million maps, charts and architectural.engineering drawings

Discussion

Unique/Interesting Features

  • You can browse by category (genealogy, places, time spans, wars/international relations, government spending, and private sector)
  • They have sections to show popular, new, and highlighted records

Pros and Cons of the System

Pros Cons
Records have tags, which makes it easier to search The site is a fixed size, and doesn’t adjust well to different sized screens
The search function tells you the format of the record You need to have a specific topic/record that you’re looking for it to be the most helpful
The search function allows you to search by the full name Not everything is available digitally, so you might have to go in person to access a record

Suggestions for Improvement

Some documentation is only available in its paper form, which would require you to be in the Nation Archives building to have access to the document. I think it would be an improvement if everything was able to be digitalized or at least transcribed so users did not have to be there in person.
